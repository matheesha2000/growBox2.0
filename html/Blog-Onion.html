Cultivation of Onions


Seed Starting:

  • Choose high-quality onion seeds from a reputable supplier. Varieties such as "Texas Early Grano" or "Walla Walla" are popular choices for home gardeners. Start onion seeds indoors 8-10 weeks before the last frost date in your area. Use seed starting trays filled with a seed starting mix. Plant onion seeds about 1/4 inch deep and 1 inch apart in rows. Keep the soil consistently moist during germination.

Germination and Seedling Care:

  • Onion seeds typically germinate within 7-14 days under optimal conditions. Thin seedlings to a spacing of 3-4 inches apart once they have developed their first true leaves to allow room for root development. Keep the soil evenly moist throughout the growing season to promote healthy growth.

Transplanting:

  • Transplant onion seedlings outdoors when they have developed several green leaves and are about pencil-thick in diameter. Choose a sunny location with well-draining soil for planting onions. Onions prefer full sun but can tolerate partial shade. Space onion transplants 4-6 inches apart in rows, with rows spaced 12-18 inches apart.

Maintenance:

  • Water onion plants regularly, providing about 1-1.5 inches of water per week. Onions prefer consistent moisture but can tolerate some dryness. Mulch around the base of the onion plants to conserve moisture, suppress weeds, and maintain even soil temperatures. Monitor for pests such as onion thrips, onion maggots, or onion root maggots, and take appropriate measures for control if necessary.

Fertilization:

  • Onions are moderate feeders and benefit from a balanced fertilizer application during the growing season to support healthy bulb development. Apply a balanced fertilizer with a formulation such as 10-10-10 or 5-10-5 according to package instructions. Side dress onion plants with fertilizer when they reach about 4-6 inches tall and again when they begin to bulb up.

Harvesting:

  • Onions are typically ready for harvest 100-150 days after planting, depending on the variety and growing conditions. Harvest onions when the tops have fallen over and begun to dry out. This indicates that the onions have reached maturity. Carefully lift the onions from the soil using a garden fork or trowel, taking care not to damage the bulbs.

Use type of soil and fertilizers:

  • Loamy soil is considered the best soil type for onion cultivation as it provides good drainage and moisture retention, promoting healthy root development and overall plant vigor. Organic compost is an excellent fertilizer for onions as it enriches the soil with organic matter and essential nutrients. Use a balanced fertilizer with equal or near-equal proportions of nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), such as 10-10-10 or 5-10-5, to provide essential nutrients for onions. Apply granular fertilizer before planting or as a side dressing during the growing season according to package instructions.
